About the Game

Mazamorf is a one-player, arcade-style maze game with a new twist: the maze keeps changing, forcing you to make quick and decisive moves. A joystick plugged into port 2 is needed to play.

You control a red-colored character who scoots around trying to collect gold pots and avoid the horrible Green Meanies. The pots appear one at a time in the corners of the maze. Collecting them increases your score and the number of walls Red can pass through. The pot collection is cumulative from game to game, and when Red has gathered 20, you advance to the next level of play.

Four Green Meanies relentlessly pursue Red through the maze. If they catch him, they latch on and sap his health until he can wriggle away. His health level starts at 100 in each game, and the longer his contact with Meanies, the more it decreases. Red should try to avoid the Meanies, but sometimes getting to the gold is more important.

Adding to the excitement of Mazamorf are the frequent changes in maze configuration that give the game its name. The pattern of change differs at each of the four levels of play, and the changes are more frequent at higher levels.

To shake off Meanies or take a shortcut to a gold pot, Red can pass through a wall, assuming the wall counter on the right side of the screen reads at least 1. It reads 4 at the start of a game, then decreases by one each time he goes through a wall. It increases by one when he grabs a gold pot.

How to Play

Objective

Collect 20 gold pots to advance to the next level. Survive all four levels. Gold pots appear one at a time in the corners of the maze. Each pot collected adds to your score and grants one additional wall pass.

Controls

InputAction
JoystickMove Red in any direction through the maze
Fire ButtonPass through an adjacent wall (costs 1 wall charge)
Be careful with the fire button. If Red is not directly next to a wall when you press it, he will move slightly and the wall counter will still decrease. Every press costs a charge whether it works or not.

Wall Passing

The wall counter starts at 4 at the beginning of each game. It decreases by one each time Red passes through a wall and increases by one whenever he collects a gold pot. Use wall passes strategically to escape Meanies or take shortcuts to gold pots.

Green Meanies

Four Green Meanies chase Red throughout the maze. They cannot be killed or stunned. If a Meanie catches Red, it latches on and continuously drains his health until he manages to wriggle free by moving away. Avoid prolonged contact at all costs.

Health System

Red's health starts at 100 each game. Contact with Green Meanies drains it steadily. A warning message appears at the bottom of the screen when health drops to 50. The game ends when health reaches zero. There are no health pickups.

Maze Changes

The maze reconfigures itself periodically during play. The frequency and pattern of these changes differs at each of the four levels, becoming more aggressive at higher levels. A wall you were relying on might vanish, and new walls might appear to block your path. Stay alert and adapt quickly.

Level Progression

Collect 20 gold pots to advance to the next level. Pot collection is cumulative across games within a session. There are four levels total, each with increasingly frequent and unpredictable maze changes.

Scoring

Points are awarded for each gold pot collected. Your current score, highest score, health level, wall counter, and remaining pots needed for the next level are all displayed on screen during play.

Publication History

1989
Mazamorf is published on the Re-Run Fun Pak 128 compilation disk by IDG Communications, Peterborough, NH. The disk is a special collection of eight new games exclusively for the Commodore 128, sold as a companion product to RUN magazine. Leonard Morris contributes three of the eight games on the disk: Cubix 2, Cubix 3, and Mazamorf. All three run in C-128 40-column mode.
1989
The Fun Pak 128 booklet introduction, written by Lou Wallace (Technical Manager, RUN Magazine), describes Mazamorf as "a new twist on the classic, maze-style arcade game" where "the mazes keep changing, so besides avoiding the nasties who are following you around, you must also try not to get stuck in the maze!"
1992
RUN magazine publishes its final issue (November/December 1992, Issue #94). IDG Communications retains the copyright to the magazine's published content.
2018
Leonard Morris passes away. Copyright interest in his original works transfers to his heirs under US copyright law.
2024
Lemon64.com user "Frightmare" catalogs Mazamorf in the Lemon64 Commodore game database (Game ID 7329) on July 27, 2024, along with five other Leonard Morris titles. The entry includes screenshots, credits, and a review rating of 5/10 — the highest score among Morris's cataloged games.
2026
This preservation page is created by Jaymes Morris, Leonard Morris's grandson and a direct descendant, with family authorization. The game is made available for play through browser-based Commodore 128 emulation as a preservation and tribute effort.

Author Credit

Mazamorf was created by Leonard Morris, who served as both programmer and graphics artist. The game was originally published in 1989 on the Re-Run Fun Pak 128 compilation disk by IDG Communications/Peterborough as a companion product to RUN magazine, an American monthly publication dedicated to Commodore home computers.

Leonard Morris was a hobbyist programmer who published at least seven games for the Commodore 128 between 1988 and 1989 across two nationally distributed magazines: RUN (IDG Communications) and COMPUTE!'s Gazette (COMPUTE! Publications, Inc.). His work appeared alongside other notable C-128 contributors in the Fun Pak 128 collection, which was specifically commissioned by RUN to address the shortage of software written for the Commodore 128's native mode.
